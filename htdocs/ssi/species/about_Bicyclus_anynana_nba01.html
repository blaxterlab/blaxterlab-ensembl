About Bicyclus anynana

The squinting bush brown butterfly, Bicyclus anynana, is a member of the remarkably speciose Mycalesine butterflies that are distributed across the Old World tropics [1]. Males have a wingspan of about 35-40 mm, while females are slightly larger (45-49 mm) [2]. Adults are found primarily in woodland habitats across East Africa (from southern Sudan in the north to Swaziland in the south), and are typically observed flying close to the ground where they feed on fallen fruit [2]. The larvae of B. anynana feed on several species of grass. It is a model organism for the study of the development, plasticity and evolution of butterfly wing patterns [2,3,4]. In addition, B. anynana is one of the only insect species to exhibit seasonal polyphenism, whereby individuals that develop during the wet season differ in both behaviour and appearance to those that develop during the dry season [1,5]. Wet-season butterflies are smaller, have shorter lifespans, are more active, and show large and conspicuous eyespots on their wings in comparison to dry season individuals [6]. Thus, B. anynana is an important system through which to study the evolution of phenotypic plasticity.

Assembly

The sequencing of the B. anynana genome was performed jointly by Edinburgh Genomics, the University of Edinburgh, Edinburgh, UK, and at the Centre for Genomic Research, the University of Liverpool, Liverpool, UK, to an overall coverage of approximately 100x. These data were QC'd and assembled by the Blaxter Lab at the University of Edinburgh. Contigs were assembled using MaSuRCA, scaffolding and gap-filling was performed with SSPACE and GapFiller. The final assembly (denoted version 0.1) has 29,616 scaffolds (188,278 contigs), an N50 of 71,168 bp and a total span of approximately 553 Mb. The GC content is 38%.

Annotation

Ab initio gene calling was performed with MAKER2, in conjuntion with transcriptomic datasets, and resulted in 14,133 gene models encoding 16,656 transcripts. These data can be veiwed and downloaded from bicyclus.org (note that assembly version 0.1 corresponds to version 0.4 on bicyclus.org).
