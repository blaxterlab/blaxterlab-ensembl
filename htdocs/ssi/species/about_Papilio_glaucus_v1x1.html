About Papilio glaucus

the Eastern Tiger Swallowtail, Papilio glaucus has interesting behavioural and morphological features at all stages in its development. Caterpillars mimic snakes and are able to emit predator-repelling terpenes[1] while adult females mimic the unpalatable pipevine swallowtail Battus philenor[2]. Sequencing of the P. glaucus genome has revealed the molecular mechanism for terpene synthesis and pupal diapause and hotspots of divergence linked to speciation[3].
