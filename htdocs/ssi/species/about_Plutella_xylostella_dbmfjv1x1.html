The diamondback moth, Plutella xylostella, is a global pest on cruciferous vegetable crops, including rapeseed, cauliflower and cabbage[1]. P. xylostella has acquired resistance to all classes of insecticide [2,3] and provides an excellent model system for understanding how insect herbivores adapt to plant defences and insecticide treatments [4]
